1. Prepare the puri: In a large bowl, mix 2 cups of atta flour (whole wheat flour), 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of water to form a dough. Knead the dough for a few minutes until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes. Roll the dough into small balls and deep fry until puffed and golden brown.
  2. Prepare the filling: In a separate bowl, mix together 1 cup of boiled and mashed potatoes, 1/2 cup of chopped onions, 1/4 cup of chopped coriander leaves, 1 teaspoon of chili powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
  3. Fill the puri: Make a small hole in the center of each puri using your thumb and fill with the potato mixture.
  4. Serve the panipuri: In a small bowl, mix together 1/2 cup of tamarind chutney, 1/2 cup of mint chutney, and 1/2 cup of water. Pour a spoonful of the mixture into each filled puri, garnish with chaat masala, and serve immediately. Enjoy!
